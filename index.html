Rahul Jain

I am a Dual Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Physics and Astronomy and the Department of Computational Mathematics, Science and Engineering at the Michigan State University in East Lansing, Michigan.
I am broadly interested in Experimental Nuclear Astrophysics and Bayesian Analysis of Nuclear Data.

A list of my scientific publications can be found here. For more information, please see my CV.

Projects

Following is the list of projects that I am currently working on:
  • Measuring the beta-decay transition strengths of exotic neutron rich isotopes for studying the crusts of accreting neutron stars.
  • Studying the impact of pycnonuclear fusion on the shallow heat source in neutron star crust models.
  • Extrapolating Nuclear Masses using Bayesian Gaussian Process Regression to create a quantified global nuclear mass model to be used in astrophysical simulations.
